Phase 1: Define & Analyze

Identifying the Bottleneck

A critical station in the assembly line was identified as a primary bottleneck. Operators were manually lifting 30kg steel bridging ramps to connect platforms to train shells, a process requiring two operators and resulting in setup times fluctuating between 5 and 30 minutes.

Using MODAPTS analysis, I quantified human motion and revealed that "Waiting for Assistance" and "Heavy Lifting" accounted for 80% of process waste.

Phase 2: Improve

The Rollable Guide System

I conceptualized a horizontal sliding mechanism to replace manual vertical lifting. Using SolidWorks, I designed a custom Aluminum frame validated through Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to simulate a 250kg load-bearing capacity.

To bypass CAPEX constraints, the final solution was fabricated using reclaimed materials from decommissioned jigs, achieving a zero-cost infrastructure upgrade.

Phase 3: Control

Standardization & Impact

The transformation was finalized by authoring a new Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) and integrating it into the plant's Visual Management board. The ergonomic score improved by 27 points, permanently moving the station from "High Risk" to "Safety Compliant".
